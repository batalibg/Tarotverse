THREE OF WANDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Preparing for what is to come.

2. Planning ahead.

3. An expanding enterprise.

4. Freelancers.

5. Creative writers.

6. Expanding horizons.

7. Seeing the big picture.

8. Being a representative.

Meanings

The Three of Wands is the card for writers, writing jobs and freelancers. It is also the card that follows the manifesting the querent did in Two of Wands, and the querent is now living a new and exciting opportunity.

Three of Wands signifies the querent is rewarded, promoted and/or has maximized their chances to succeed. The enterprise is expanding.

In a love reading the Three of Wands speaks of the earlier stages of a relationship.

In a career reading it suggests careers in writing, project management or freelancing.

Personality Types

This is someone with great inner strength and power. They know they are in charge of their own destiny. They can be arrogant however, and may act entitled. They thrive on responsibility and take charge with a noble attitude.

They love inserting new ideas into their routine, especially if it has a competitive touch. This is someone self-confident and optimistic. Their enthusiasm remains high as long as they can see the fruits of their labor.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Overwhelmed by demands.

2. Unable to complete a project.

3. A toxic environment.

4. Disappointments.

Meanings

The Three of Wands reversed indicates that there are delays in rewards and achievements. The querent might be out of their league, or unable to cope with demands. The environment might be toxic, which makes it hard for growth.

People might not like what you are doing. Disappointments are likely. Conceit is also a possibility. Psychologically this card reversed hints at arrogance and rudeness.

Personality Types

The Three of Wands reversed reports someone with a hard time breaking free from their habits and routines. Their habits aren't healthy, and they need to break free from their toxic patterns. This is someone quite disagreeable, and they often find themselves in a defensive position.

They need to learn about other cultures and ways of life, as their world is usually small and unappealing. They tend to exist within their own uncomfortable comfort zones.

Symbolism

The cliff edge signals being ready to leap. The wand in his left hand represents his planning ahead. The two wands behind him symbolize the work and research he has put into his vision.

The yellow sky and sea suggests his intellect and knowledge, and facing the sun implies facing life.

Summary

The Three of Wands shows up when it is time to nurture what you've poured love and effort into. This is not a time to be complacent.

Protect and expand on what you worked so hard to build, and the foundation will be the substance you need to assure future security and success.

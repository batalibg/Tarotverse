EIGHT OF CUPS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Following your dream.

2. Seeking deeper meaning.

3. Focusing on the spiritual rather than the material.

4. Being emotionally distant.

5. Abandoning the mainstream way of life.

6. Changing your priorities. Walking away from something.

7. Having few friends. Being a loner.

Meanings

The Eight of Cups is the card for turning your back on something to go and follow your own dream. If the Eight of Cups shows up with the Hermit you will most likely want to go on this path alone. The Eight of Cups occurs when it is time to seek what is truly satisfying for the querent.

In a love reading the Eight of Cups denotes a time where relationships should take second place in the querent’s life. The querent knows he/she must fulfill their own needs before any relationship can deepen. The querent needs to cut lose, go into the wilderness, and return a new person. The wilderness represents the place in the querent’s life where everything is put on the line in order to achieve something more meaningful.

The Eight of Cups can mean casual work supporting the querent while on a journey. It can also mean pursuing what the querent has always wanted to do, but was too afraid to do. When the Eight of Cups is drawn it means the fear is gone, and the desire to live a new life has triumphed.

Personality Types

The Eight of Cups indicates someone who seeks success and happiness away from the material world. They abandon the mainstream way of life in search for substance. They know there is something better out there and they want to get to it. They are often on a deep spiritual journey. They’ve changed their priorities and many speak of them as someone who has changed all together. They have turned their life around in some way, and have grown up emotionally to the point that their friends and family hardly recognise them anymore.

Eight of Cups personality types are often very introverted as they are seeking inner meaning and answers rather than earthy pleasures. They can at times seem to be unattainable, as they are very private people. They need to persevere, as giving up to external pressure will be a huge step backwards. Eight of Cups-type people need to do what is right for them rather than what is right for everyone else.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Abandoning your dreams.

2. Sacrificing your happiness.

3. Feeling miserable.

Meanings

The Eight of Cups reversed indicates that the querent has given up their dream to surrender to an uncomfortable, loveless and miserable reality. The querent might have surrendered their life to please someone else, like selling their business to work in a job they dislike. The Eight of Cups sometimes shows up reversed when the querent feels completely lost in the world, and they have issues with shame and guilt. There is meaningless change and journey without purpose. The querent has stopped growing.

Personality Types

The Eight of Cups reversed imlies someone who feels old and useless. They believe they are over the hill and that they have nothing left to offer the world. Everything seems to be too difficult and hopeless for them. It is easier for them to give in to demands of others than to focus on their own needs and desires. This is someone with a tendency to escape through prescription drugs or, in some cases, illicit drug addictions.

They are battling lethargy and apathy, and they believe things are a lot worse than they actually are. Eight of Cups reversed people tend to go towards the path of least resistance, as any challenge seems to overwhelm them. However the lack of growth in their lives will end up hurting them in the long haul, and they'll have to participate whether they like it or not. This is someone who is hiding in the background and is at times hardly noticed. They feel very emotionally misunderstood.

Symbolism

The Eight Cups symbolises material goods. The man is walking away from the cups, representing turning away from the physical world to seek something more meaningful. The terrain in front of him is uneven, illustrating new challenges and opportunities for learning. The red cloak infers the querent’s passion for this journey.

The water separating the land signifies the struggle between the subconscious and conscious mind. The mountain in front of him refers to new stability, if he stays on his path. The moon symbolises the querent's dreams.

Summary

The Eight of Cups occurs when it is time to go and chase something big. There are no guarantees where it will take you, but you can’t keep doing what you are doing either. Something’s gotta give, and it is most likely going to be the routines you are dreading each day. Make small steps if your dreams scare you. Time will pass either way, so you might as well get started now.

TWO OF WANDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Making an intuitive decision.

2. Being original.

3. Creating something new and exciting.

4. Being strong minded and confident about your abilities.

5. Finding your place in the world.

6. Knowing what you want.

7. Getting ahead.

Meanings

The Two of Wands is about manifesting your goals using the will and strength of your mind. It asks you to go on a journey to find your place in the world. It is a card that speaks to what you truly desire. The other cards in the spread will tell you how you can go about acquiring this.

You might have to weigh your options and choose the one that is best for you. This card often shows up when the querent knows exactly what they want, and they feel strongly about making it happen.

In a love reading the Two of Wands suggests dating and searching for love, often online.

In a career question it relates to leadership, legal or intellectual work.

Personality Types

The Two of Wands persona is a leader with a vision for the future. This is someone who will conquer their dreams. They make things happen, and are not afraid to take charge of a situation. Other people follow them gladly, as they appear confident. They are courageous and don't shy away from conflict if anyone stands in their way.

They are competitive and want to be better, richer, or stronger than others. They almost always get their way. This is someone charming and convincing, and people usually take to them instantly.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Indecisive.

2. On hold.

3. Copying others.

Meanings

The Two of Wands reversed reports difficulties in making decisions, possibly due to the fear of making the wrong choice. Upright, this card shows someone with strong willpower, reversed a weak will is suggested. The querent is easily led, rather than being a leader.

Personality Types

The Two of Wands reversed flags someone with a tendency to either rush ahead before they have thought it through properly, or who second guesses themselves so nothing gets done. They tend to be grumpy and moody. They have a hard time controlling their temper and have many outbursts during the day.

When it's time to go to bed they are totally exhausted from the emotional roller-coaster they take themselves on. They have a hard time trusting their own decisions and if you ask them what they want, they have little clue. If they do know what they want, they lack the insight into how to make it a reality. Ideas often live and die in their heads.

Symbolism

The globe in the man's right hand stands for having the world in your hand. The wand on his right represents what he has put on hold to achieve his dream. The new plan is symbolized by the wand he is holding in his left hand. The man's red hat and cape infer his passion. His orange tunic reflects his enthusiasm.

On his left side is an image of a rose, a lily and a cross. The rose embodies beauty. The lily means hope. The cross symbolizes faith. The man stands on top of a castle overlooking the terrain and the ocean, illustrating a great vision.

Summary

When the Two of Wands shows up in a reading you must make your mind up and trust in your intuition. Be strong minded about something and push forward. Be a leader. Someone has to do it, and it should be you.

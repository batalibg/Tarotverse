FIVE OF CUPS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Grief and sorrow.

2. Suffering, pain and loss.

3. Regrets and disappointments.

4. Being emotionally unsatisfied.

5. Being hard to please.

6. Focusing on the negative.

7. Feeling unloved.

Meanings

The Five of Cups is the card for regrets and disappointments, especially in love. A relationship might not have turned out the way you would have liked it to. The querent is grieving the loss, and is unable to see what is still there. All is not lost though. In a work related query you might not be getting the recognition you deserve, or someone else might be getting rewarded for your work. The other cards in the spread will tell you in what regard your life is being affected.

In a love reading the Five of Cups speaks of disappointment. A relationship did not turn out the way the querent was hoping for. It can mean the relationship is ending, or that an aspect of the relationship has ended. Perhaps the querent is missing what was, and is not sure about the new phase of the relationship. If single, the Five of Cups can mean the querent is feeling lonely.

The querent might not think there is any hope for a happy relationship, and maybe they are settling for less than what they deserve.

In a career reading the Five of Cups signifies work that isn't really satisfy the querent. The querent might have a great potential to achieve in their profession, but for some reason is unable to blossom. It can also mean that the querent feels isolated at work.

Personality Types

The Five of Cups-type is someone who is living with disappointment and regrets. This person is hard to please. They look at the negative in every situation. They also find it hard to begin something new, as they seem to always be stuck in the past. Emotionally they are finding it hard to cope, and they are often playing the blame game. They have little self-love and rarely look after their health and well-being. They need to deal with the loss and grief in their life, and realize that life still has plenty to offer them.

This is someone with a victim mentality, focusing on their regrets, loss or failures. This is someone who is an excellent friend when another's life is in turmoil, as they relate to what that feels like. As soon as your life starts looking up and you embrace positivity, they turn on you and accuse you of changing, and of being a poor friend. They can only join you in your pain, not in your happiness.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Old grief.

2. Feeling hopeless.

3. Abuse.

Meanings

The Five of Cups reversed indicates disappointment in love and emotional matters. The two Cups that are still upright spill out when this card occurs reversed, which denotes the querent might be emotionally drained, usually by relationships. There is danger of being taken advantage of, or even abused, by the people around you - so be careful. The Five of Cups sometimes shows up reversed when the querent is so heartbroken they are unable to love again.

Personality Types

The Five of Cups indicates someone who has hit rock bottom emotionally. Their negativity has rippled out and ruined what was still a good thing in their life. It can can be very difficult to be around a Five of Cups reversed personality type. They are often mentally unstable and they take their pain out on innocent people, ruining their day and trying to get them into trouble.

This is someone with lots of skeletons in the closet and a string of failed ventures that haunt them. They have an all or nothing attitude to life which ends up working against them. This is someone who feels very sorry for themselves, and believes no one understands or appreciates them.

Symbolism

The black cloak symbolises grief. The three fallen cups with spilt contents represent loss. The two cups still standing are the relationships that remain. The figure has his back to the two cups standing and is facing the three fallen cups, signifying he is focusing on loss. The white bridge shows hope and support. The castle illustrates home and security, thus the loss is emotional rather than material.

Summary

Emotional dissatisfaction seen in the Four of Cups intensifies in the Five of Cups. The querent might feel like running away and starting over, but that might not be the answer. Getting away can feel great - just make sure you know when to stop running. Grief and sorrow are part of life, and if we work through these emotions we experience growth. The Five of Cups sometimes shows up when the querent experiences heartbreak in personal relationships. When the Five of Cups is reversed the heartbreak is prolonged.

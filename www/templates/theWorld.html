THE WORLD

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Structure and meaning.

2. Accomplishments.

3. The boundaries that direct us while we grow.

4. Traveling to another part of the world.

5. Teaching the world something you are an expert in.

6. Rules and regulations that assist in making the world a safer place.

7. Fulfillment.

8. Completion.

9. Doing what we desire most.

Meanings

The World is the final Major Arcana card and it represents fulfillment and successful completion of a cycle. You know your place in the world, and your life lessons have made you smart and accomplished. The World shows up when the world is ready for you, and wants what you have to offer. You might have to broaden your horizon and move to where your dreams will come true. This card often shows up when there is travel involved to a far away place. Your sail just got some wind behind it, so make the most of it.

The difference between the Fool's journey and journeying with the World is that where the Fool is inexperienced and relying on beginners luck, the World has experience, wisdom, knowledge and experience. The World shows up to remind you that you mean the world to someone, and because of this you are infinitely significant.

In a love reading The World denotes a relationship that stays equally strong in both sickness and health. If the querent is single the World can mean finding love with someone from a different country.

In a career reading The World signifies careers in government and politics. The World can also entail overseas jobs.

Personality Types

Confident, wise, worldly and full of life experience, The World is someone with substance. They are not afraid to put in effort and get their hands dirty, and because of this they are often quickly promoted in their jobs. They excel in teaching/training positions. This character is someone who is confident in most situations.

Their positive attitude and skills often take them to overseas locations where they share their knowledge. At the same time they love to learn - in fact they have to keep learning to feel alive. They are often very well-educated, as well as possessing plenty of practical knowledge. These people can be relied upon, and are often in trusted positions.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Incomplete.

2. Isolation.

3. Undisciplined.

Meanings

The World reversed indicates staying at home, secluded within comfort zones. Ventures are abandoned and projects remain incomplete. The querent is unable to finish what they started.

There is a false view of the world that often stops the person from getting involved and enjoying what the world has to offer. There is a fear of the outside world. The World reversed denotes stillness rather than movement, or worse, going backwards rather than forward and being stuck in the past.

Personality Types

The World reversed purposes someone who is seeing the world through the eyes of fear. They are often isolating themselves, suffer social anxiety and have a general lack of confidence. They are inexperienced and immature, and they find it hard to finish what they've started. This is someone who shies away from challenges and responsibility, or who gets stuck in work roles with no future aspects of promotions.

They like to study, but when it comes to implementing their knowledge in the workplace they fall short. Sometimes they stay in the student role so they can avoid responsibility all together. They are afraid of leaving their comfort zone. Around people they feel awkward, even scared. These sorts of people talk themselves out of trying new things and meeting new people. Abandonment issues and anxieties are often the underlying issue.

Astrology

The World is ruled by Saturn. Saturn brings structure and meaning to the world, it knows the limits to time and matter. This way it brings definition to our lives. It rules our boundaries, responsibilities and commitments. Saturn helps us understand the world around us through governments and their resources, discipline, structure, rules and regulations. Saturn shows that it is our sense of responsibility and our limitations that define us and give us boundaries. The World card represents Saturn at its best: our duty, honor, work, discipline and traditions - the boundaries that direct us while we grow.

Symbolism

The human head represents Aquarius, the lion is Leo, the ox as Taurus and the eagle stands for Scorpio. These four signs are the fixed signs in the zodiac. They also represent the four corners of the earth, and the four elements.

The World signals that we are viewing the cosmic throne. The dancing figure relates cosmic consciousness and the world force. The green wreath represents the sensual world. In each hand she holds a wand equal to each other. The two equal wands are the twin pillars of duality. They have become tools for the creative expression of the soul.

The purple garment embodies the balance between the stability of blue and the passion of red. The red ribbon forms the infinity symbol, bespeaking endless opportunities, rewards and happiness. This card recalls the Anima Mundi (latin for World Soul) which is an intrinsic connection between all living things on the planet. In a reading the World represents what is truly desired.

Summary

The World expresses accomplishments, structures and meaning. It speaks of what is truly important to the querent - what they are here to teach the World. In a reading the World can mean the querent is about to travel to a far away place. The querent travels to seek experience, knowledge and wisdom. When the World shows up reversed the querent is afraid to make their mark on the world.

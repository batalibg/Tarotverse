THE EMPEROR

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Creating order out of chaos.

2. Leadership and authority.

3. Making your own rules and having others obeying them.

4. Being your own boss.

5. Structure, logic and reason.

6. Energy, force and drive.

7. Being fearless.

Meanings

The Emperor card represents masculine energy - the ruler and head of the household. It can represent leaders of companies, organizations or communities. The Emperor is an authority figure who creates a solid foundation for others to build upon. The Emperor creates order out of chaos. The Emperor uses logic rather than intuition, and he prefers well thought out plans above impulsive action. The Emperor has strong values and beliefs and is not likely to budge. This card can often represent big companies and/or government organisations. In a reading the Emperor often shows up when it is time for the querent to make a fresh start. This card advises that self-control and discipline are the best way forward. Make a commitment and honor it.

In a love reading the Emperor denotes a relationship in which the couple often works together to build their own empire. In a career reading the Emperor signifies self-employment or high-level leadership jobs.

Personality Types

The Emperor archetype is someone competitive and ambitious. This is the aggressive alpha-male who naturally gains power over others. Honest and offensive, Emperor type people don’t apologize for their behaviour. They are confident and sure of their own worth, ready to take charge of any situation. Confrontational by nature they often exhibit anger issues and have a big ego. They love being in charge. They also love getting lots of attention from others.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Chaos.

2. Poor leadership.

3. Lack of energy.

Meanings

The Emperor reversed translates to someone with a childish streak, inconsistent and unpredictable. If it represents the querent it can mean something is blocking the energy that gives the person authority.

Rather than being respected, you might find that people are dishonoring you. There is a lack of control and order. The Emperor reversed can indicate a time when chaos and havoc rules.

Personality Types

The Emperor reversed expresses someone who feels dissatisfied, and is unhappy with their life situation. They often lack confidence. This is someone who is absent or elusive. Dreamy and impulsive, they come across as highly unpredictable. This card often shows up reversed when you are dealing with teenagers.

Astrology

The Emperor is connected with Mars and is ruled by Aries. Aries is the first sign of the Zodiac and denotes leadership and initiative. Mars energizes all of the other planets in the zodiac. Mars governs the masculine element and is known as the warrior planet, it covers sexual powers, courage, energy, action, protectiveness, and valor. Mars also governs sharp instruments, fire and anything combustible, and if Mars is too energetic, emotional outbursts could erupt. If harmonious, Mars assists with endurance and determination. Mars is also connected to the Tower.

Symbolism

The throne embodies being in power. The ram heads symbolize power, force, drive, energy, virility, protection and fearlessness. There are four rams heads carved on his stone chair, invoking a stable energy.

The Emperor is armored under his robe, reflecting his stature as a leader and ruler. The cold and rocky landscape represents the Emperor’s discipline. In his right hand he holds an ankh, which is the Egyptian symbol for life. In his left hand he holds an orb, symbolising a globus cruciger, which is a symbol for emperors, kings and archangels.

The Emperor’s long white hair and beard indicates that he has wisdom and experience, as well as a high social status. The orange background and his red clothing signals his passion and drive.

The Emperor announces leadership and authority. The querent will achieve the best result when implementing structure and logic to their projects when the Emperor shows up in their spread. This card can also signify government, government agencies or large instances of cooperation. In a career reading the Emperor often is present when the querent will be most satisfied working for themselves.

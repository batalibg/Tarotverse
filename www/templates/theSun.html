THE SUN

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Enlightenment.

2. Joy and happiness.

3. A child.

4. A holiday. A warm place.

5. Being accepted. Self-realization.

6. Optimism.

7. Great health and vitality.

8. Victory and accomplishments.

Meanings

The Sun embodies success, joy, sunshine, day, warmth and happiness. This card shows up when life is sunny and you are enjoying your time with the people you love. Life is simple rather than complicated. Relationships are blossoming and you are feeling loved. Sometimes the Sun represents a much welcomed child. Have a look at the other cards to see where the source of happiness is coming from. It might be coming from within, from a child, success at work, harmony with family or a relationship.

The Sun can also represent a much-needed holiday. The Sun is the BIG YES card in a positive sense, it will give you what you want and desire: you are encouraged to apply for that job, go on that holiday, ask out your crush on a date, connect with family, or do that art class. The Sun assures you that if you do, it will pay off for you. You will not be rejected. The Sun is the card for acceptance.

In a love reading the Sun denotes a new relationship full of potential. The Sun describes a relationship which is sociable and fun. If the querent is single the Sun can mean they will soon experience love at first sight. This card can also mean a holiday romance.

In a career reading the Sun signifies leadership jobs. The Sun can also mean a fun job.

Personality Types

Sun card people are optimistic, happy and confident - someone who is always looking for ways to enjoy life and experience joy. This is someone with a youthful and carefree nature, no matter what age. They love new beginnings, seeing new places and learning new skills. They never grow old.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Poor logic.

2. Sadness.

3. Low vitality.

Meanings

The Sun reversed foretells that new beginnings, success and happiness is being blocked. Success is blocked due to reaching the wrong conclusions and having bad reasoning and poor logic. Things are frustrating and unclear. There might be some problems with pregnancy, or raising young children. The Sun reversed can form an eclipse, which means there are endings approaching, and some previously active mode or method is outdated.

There is no longer excitement and joy, just feelings of the mundane and mediocrity. The querent might be afraid of new beginnings, of success and being in the spotlight. This person might feel more comfortable in the shadow of their own potential. Ignorance is winning. No one is listening to or thinking of the future generations.

Personality Types

The Sun reversed marks someone who has low vitality, is arrogant, melodramatic or vain. This is someone with low energy, poor self-esteem and with a pessimistic outlook on life. They often make poor decisions about their future.

Astrology

The Sun is ruled by the sun, the ruler of your purpose, character, will, determination, heart, health, vitality and consciousness. The Sun represents the ego and how you view yourself. It rules our basic identity and our self-realization. The Sun represents reason rather than instinct. When well aspected the Sun rules happiness, enjoyment, integrity and strength of will and purpose. If poorly aspected it denotes poor vitality, low energy, lack of belief in one's self, poor judgment, arrogance, melodrama or vanity.

Symbolism

The child stands for innocence. The feather on the child's head is the same feather worn by the Fool. The feather is connecting the child with the Sun and with the Fool, connoting inner light. The banner is a victory banner, signaling accomplishments and victory. The child's posture enacts joy and happiness.

The child holds the banner in her left hand, suggesting the power of the subconscious. The child rides the horse without saddle and reins, displaying trust and harmony. The white horse embodies strength, beauty and purity. The sun represents life and happiness, warmth and goodness.

The 22 sun-rays are the major arcana. The straight rays represent action, and the wavy rays represent vibration and flow. The four sunflowers are the four suits in the minor arcana and the fourfold world. Sunflowers represent love and happiness.

SIX OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Moving towards a better future.

2. Finding solutions.

3. Changing location.

4. Problem solving.

5. Feeling a bit low emotionally.

6. Being sentimental.

7. Moving in the right direction.

8. The need for change.

Meanings

The Six of Swords is about crossing a troubled sea and entering calmer water. This is the card for finding solutions and implementing them, often bringing other people with you in the process. The other cards in the spread will tell you which areas you are making these changes. Sometimes it shows up when people are taking physical travel away from stress.

If this card shows up with Eight of Wands it can denote going on a holiday. If it shows up with Nine of Pentacles it often mean moving to a beautiful place. The Six of Swords with the Chariot can signify a quick leap forward in the right direction, so don’t lose sight of the bigger picture and don’t let go of control.

In a love reading the Six of Swords denotes meeting someone online, or a long distance relationship. It can also mean a relationship where the couple has worked on their relationship and has come through the other side.

In a career reading it signifies science, problem-solving, computer and travel jobs.

Personality Types

The Six of Swords indicates someone who is very good at problem solving and implementing new strategies and systems. They are very intelligent people who can master skills in many different environments. They continuously seek to create a better world, and are impartial and fair. They often succeed in personal goals and are always ready to go on a new adventure, learning new skills and meeting new people as they go.

These sorts of people love to invest in themselves and they often take their expertise to humanitarian causes and non-profit organisations, where they work hard towards building a better tomorrow.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Problems staying afloat.

2. Losing important information.

3. Mental confusion.

Meanings

While the Six of Swords is reversed it forecasts travel plans being cancelled or delayed. It also means difficulty in problem solving, especially with mental tasks like math or science. The swords fall out of the boat when the Six of Swords shows up reversed, denoting the danger of losing valuable information needed.

Make sure you back up and protect your data. This card sometimes shows up reversed when the someone is stealing someone else’s intellectual properties, homework and/or ideas.

Personality Types

The Six of Swords reversed implies someone who has problems staying afloat. They might even have problems with authority figures or following rules. They believe rules are there to be broken. There are problems with getting stuck and lost, and there are issues with time management. This is someone who is constantly asking for other people’s help and handouts.

They seem to always be in deep water with little effort, and few resources to get them out of trouble. They often drain their friends and family, who in the end get fed up with them. When that happens they hook on to someone else, often an unknowing do-gooder.

Symbolism

The turbulent water on the boat’s right side marks trouble. The calm water on the boat’s left side signals the ending of troubles. The man steering them across is standing upright, alluding to planning ahead. The woman and the child sit in front of him. The woman’s head is covered, indicating humbleness.

The child on her left side stands for new beginnings. All the six swords are pointing downwards, signifying mental rest and non-activity. The six swords are in front of the people in this card represent their boundaries.

Summary

The Six of Swords embodies the mental workout that will lead to a better and brighter future. Once you learn the necessary skills you will have the security you need. It can also speak to travel, as traveling always entails learning in some way or another, and this card stands for the joy of that learning process.

If you want a brighter future the Six of Swords will get you there by bringing about change through learning. The most prevalent mental skills seen in Six of Swords are computer skills, musical skills and language skills.

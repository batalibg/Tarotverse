KNIGHT OF PENTACLES

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Change of work.

2. Ambition.

3. A move due to work.

4. Putting in the effort. Never giving up-attitude.

5. Being practical.

6. Work hard and play hard.

7. Seeing result after working hard at it.

8. More of the same effort will yield results.

Meanings

The Knight of Pentacles connotes being of service and doing the actual work (in contrast to the Page of Pentacles who enjoys daydreaming about the idea rather than actually living it). The Knight of Pentacle is comfortable with routine, is efficient and conservative. If this card represents you in a reading it often means you will be trusted with more responsibility and accountability.

Do not shy away from this, but make sure you accomplish your work dutifully and with attention to detail. There is no need to make any major changes to your approach, as this card says more of the same effort will do, and following through with your well worked out routine is the way to go.

In a love reading the Knight of Pentacles signals finding love when you are either changing residence or changing jobs. If in a relationship it shows the couple is working hard for a better future. They might be renovating or investing in property, or they are working hard in their careers to earn more money for their future.

In a career reading the Knight of Pentacles speaks of changes in a work routine. A new job will present itself, which will be a move in the right direction.

Personality Types

The Knight of Pentacles type is someone who is usually very kind and understanding. They are considerate and have others best interest at heart. They are grounded, trustworthy and reliable, dedicated to service and sacrifice. These people are both logical and creative at once, they know when it is time to follow a strict routine and when it is time to make the necessary changes.

They have a love for property and get excited by the thought of owning a portfolio of investments. They love business and are very practical people. They are family oriented and are often great providers.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Envy.

2. Sloppiness.

3. Stagnation in career.

Meanings

When the Knight of Pentacles is reversed reflects sloppiness, bitterness and envy. This is someone who is sucking up to those who can help them climb the ladder to wealth and success, but will do nothing for those who havenâ€™t got money or connections.

This is someone who is also tight with money. The Knight of Pentacles sometimes shows up reversed when there is stagnation and frustration in career and financial matters. If not a person, this card indicates a problem in travels, especially in journeys by foot, hiking or cycling. Double check your gear. Getting lost is a strong possibility.

Personality Types

The Knight of Pentacles reversed personality is someone ready for a change, but too scared to make a move. They desperately long for a holiday, and feel bitter about putting effort into their work and home. They have issues with breaking out of unhealthy habits and are immature for their age.

Such people have terrible time management skills and often prioritize poorly. This is someone who will spend as much time as possible playing games on their phone or on game consoles, talking about things that have little value. They tend to spend more time with the TV than with their family.

Symbolism

The heavy horse symbolizes the knights efforts and his grounded nature. The road indicates his ambition and where he is heading. The knight is holding a coin up with his right hand, insinuating earning a wage. His gaze is ahead, marking his vision. His horse stands on a hill, inferring that he is contemplating a possible change.

Summary

The knights are all about changes, so when the Knight of Pentacles turns up in your reading it is an agent of change in your work. Often this card speaks to change of work, either as a conscious choice or as a inevitable turn of events. The Knight of Pentacles also suggests moving into a new living situation.

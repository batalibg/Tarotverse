TWO OF CUPS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Your other half. Deep love between two people.

2. Making a connection with someone. Intimacy.

3. Spiritual love.

4. A unique and special friendship.

5. Giving and receiving love and support.

6. Healing.

7. Spending quality time with your partner.

Meanings

The Two of Cups is the card for being in love and being in a close relationship. There is real intimacy in the relationship, and you know and understand each other. There is healing taking place in this relationship. If the question is about work, then you will find friendships and possibly even love in the workplace. You might find that you love going to work because of the people you are working with, and not just because of work itself.

Tips for when the Two of Cups shows up in a reading: this card often speaks of the querent’s second half. The querent is often working behind the scenes to make room for their partner. Help them find ways to enjoy their relationship in a healthy way.

In a love reading the Two of Cups denotes a deep friendship within the relationship, and that the relationship has center stage in the querent’s life. The relationship is their number 1 priority.

In a career reading this card signifies work in hospitality, wine tasting, therapy healing. The Two of Cups can also mean you will find love at work.

Personality Types

The Two of Cups persona is someone who has a friendly personality. They often only feel happy once they are in a deep and meaningful relationship. They are always looking for true love and once they have it they feel at peace with themselves and the world.

These sorts of people will do almost anything for love. They make sure they look beautiful and have a great career so that they will be attractive to their partners. The motivation behind why they put in all the effort is so that they will feel worthy of finding and keeping love.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Abandonment.

2. Separation.

3. Communication hiccup.

Meanings

When the Two of Cups is reversed it reflects being afraid of love and relationships. The querent might be dreaming of love, but when it comes down to actually having it, fear of rejection and abandonment gets in the way. This card sometimes shows up reversed when there is a separation or break in a relationship, or a communication hiccup has caused conflict or distance between couples. Look for surrounding cards to find the clue for why this is occurring. Next to the Three of Swords it can indicate a complete breakdown in communication. Adjacent to the Three of Cups reversed it can indicate adultery. Aside the Three of Wands it can indicate the couple has grown apart.

Personality Types

The Two of Cups reversed is someone who feels it is safer to dream about love than actually have it. They dream about the perfect partner, but feel deep down that this is unachievable to attain. They don't think themselves worthy of love. They believe no one wants them. These people are afraid of losing themselves in relationships. They can only commit half way before they get scared and run. When they do enter into a relationship they need a lot of reassuring from their partner that everything is good.

They also need plenty of time on their own as being together is draining and exhausting for a them. They need to take a step back and enter into the energy of the Ace of Cups until they are connected to themselves. They need to love themselves more. The Two of Cups often shows up reversed when the querent is in a relationship without loving themselves first. They are scared of abandonment.

Symbolism

The five streams pouring out of the cup represent the five senses: sight, smell, hearing, taste and touch. The cloud stands for movement and the changeable.

The sky symbolizes the unchangeable, eternity and the Infinite. The dove represents the cosmic nature within us all. The water represents our spirit in our human temporary form. The sea alludes to our group consciousness. The lotus flower signals good fortune and pure spirit.

Summary

While the Ace of Cups spoke about the potential for love to enter your life if you are open to receive, the two of Cups speaks of that love blossoming. It is true love, and a real connection. It is wonderful and scary, and most definitely worth it. Life without love can be harsh. We all have so much potential in us, and it is often hidden until someone special sheds their loving light on us. Love makes us better people. Love makes us ask the big questions. Love makes everything worth while.

THE MAGICIAN

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Realizing your potential.

2. Believing in yourself.

3. Using your talents.

4. Being in charge of your own destiny.

5. Being able to sell yourself, sell your ideas and sell a product.

6. Using old skills in a new field

7. Re-inventing yourself.

Meanings

The Magician represents your ability to communicate clearly, to ‘sell’ yourself and to be innovative. The Magician has all the tools and resources available to manifest his desired outcome, so it is a good card to get if you want to create. The Magician is skillful and confident in his abilities, and he has the gift of the gab, able to pull the people in that he needs for his desired outcome to take place.

In a love reading the Magician often describes your most compatible soul mate.

In a career question it relates to jobs in sales and communications.

Personality Types

The Magician personae are people who can manifest and create almost anything they put their minds to. This is the mind over matter card, and it indicates people who have amazing abilities to use their skill and knowledge in ways that serve them. They believe anything is possible. They are often very creative people who are used to taking the initiative. They are great at selling themselves. They can sell sand in the desert if they had to. They dream big and successfully move in any direction they choose. This card speaks to your mind and your intellect, and to use your intelligence to create and manifest.

Play smart or don't play at all is the energy of this card. It also tells you to remember your spirit, and that your actions have consequences for your spirit, as well as your physical body. It is a card for innovation. If you draw this card it often means your will is pushing you in a direction that is exciting and stimulating. You might find yourself inventing something completely new or doing something in a new way. Whatever you do you'll be quite the trend setter as you seem to have magical abilities and everything you touch turns to gold.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Miscommunication.

2. A fraud.

3. Lack of self-belief.

Meanings

The Magician reversed suggests empty promises, false possibilities, a con man, misunderstandings and a lack of substance to make possibilities become a reality. The possible is becoming impossible when the Magician is upside down.

If this card shows up reversed for you in a reading it is not a great time for you to begin something new.

You might find that no one is taking you seriously when this card shows up reversed, or that the people and situations in your life are more like a joke than real opportunities.

Personality Types

This is the audacious con man. They don't believe they can have what they want, and use manipulation to try to get their way. They are lacking in confidence, and are either misunderstood, or they misunderstand others. They can have problems with learning, and are burying their heads in the sand. They procrastinate and are often overly critical of both themselves and others. They are quite messy people and their ideas live and die in their heads. When they contact you it is only because they want something from you, and it usually is your money.

Astrology

The Magician is connected to the planet Mercury. Mercury is the planet for communication, short distance travel, quick-moving connections (phones, internet, computers, etc.) Mercury is the planet of the intellect, it is linked with cleverness, dexterity and wittiness. It speaks of how we learn, how the mind works and how we communicate. Mercury governs the senses. Mercury is easily swayed by emotions, and there might be problems seeing the truth of the matter. Mercury also rules Gemini, the sign of The Lovers and Virgo, the sign of The Hermit.

Symbolism

The infinity symbol above the Magician’s head represents being connected to Source and the timeless. The belt forms an ouroboros, a circular symbol of time formed by a snake biting its tail. The Magician stands in a garden of roses and lilies, the red flowers are equated to the masculine and the white flowers the feminine.

His wand is raised to the heavens and his finger is pointing to earth, which shows the Magician using his spiritual powers in conjunction with his practical abilities to create. His pose exhibits the Hermetic axiom ‘as above, so below’, meaning Heaven should be manifested on Earth.

His table suggests being able to sell and putting an offer on the table. The four elements represented by a pentacle, a cup, a sword and a wand - the resources the Magician has at his disposal. Three symbols also appear to be carved on the edge of the table: the first being a seascape representing water, the second the element of fire and the third is a dove.

The dove is the spirit. The yellow background indicates that this is an intellectual card, and using your wit, mind and communication skills is going to be favorable. This card signals willpower, initiations and beginnings.

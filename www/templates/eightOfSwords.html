EIGHT OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Confused about what to do.

2. Fear of failure.

3. Afraid of making the wrong decisions.

4. Unable to move forward. Being stuck.

5. Wanting to be rescued.

6. Not trusting in yourself enough.

7. Being kept in the dark.

8. Lacking direction.

9. Working more efficiently on your own.

Meanings

The Eight of Swords communicates being stuck and unable to move on due to confusion. Often people draw this card when their own instincts are being over ruled by other people. If the Eight of Swords shows up with the Devil then the querent's fear is stopping them from seeing clearly. The Devil can also represent incredibly strong-minded people who are forcing their beliefs on you, leaving you doubting yourself.

The Eight of Swords combined with the Seven of Swords forecasts that you are being kept in the dark about certain matters, and looking to the other cards in the spread will tell you what these interferences are. The Eight of Swords often shows up when the querent is meant to hold off making any decisions, as the querent does not yet have all the information, and the best outcome will be catalyzed by outside forces.

In a love reading the Eight of Swords can mean that the querent is just about to meet the love of their life. If the querent is in a relationship the Eight of Swords indicates the querent wants to take the relationship to the next level, but the time is not yet right to move forward.

In a career reading the Eight of Swords suggests a career working with people who have speech impediments, such as speech pathologists, special aid therapists and support workers.

Personality Types

The Eight of Swords type is someone who is very cerebral. They often get lost in their heads and are unable to see the bigger picture. It is not uncommon that they get fixated on unimportant details and issues. Their prioritization is often poor and they become confused as a result.

They often feel misunderstood, and make snap judgments. This is someone who has a pattern of misconception and misapprehension. They focus on appearance and image over substance and truth.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Overly self-conscious.

2. Wanting to go back in time.

3. Escaping in head-space.

Meanings

The Eight of Swords reversed indicates a person who is unable to move forward due to self doubts, fear or isolation. The person knows the way forward, yet chooses to stay put, escaping in their own heads. This card often shows up when the querent wants to return to an earlier stage of their life. They are unhappy in their current situation and want what they had in the past.

Personality Types

The Eight of Swords reversed type someone who is prone to self-sabotage. They are trying to redeem themselves but find it hard to move forward. They don’t always know the difference between right and wrong, and they often take the easy way rather than the right way.

This is someone who believes the truth will hurt them. They have very poor self-esteem, and often bury their heads in the sand. They are afraid of success and they don’t believe in their own power.

Symbolism

The eight swords pointing downright symbolise the woman’s fears that are causing her to stay passive. Three of them are to the woman’s right, referring to her fear of becoming broken-hearted (see Three of Swords) and the five Swords are to her left embodying her fear of becoming defeated (see Five of Swords). The rope and blind fold illustrate her confusion and inactive state.

The gray sky connotes lack of hope, possibly even depression. She is standing all alone far away from the town in the distance, reflecting her isolation. Her feet are on uneven ground, signifying her fear of moving forward.

Summary

The Eight of Swords often shows up in a reading when someone has a learning difficulty, a mental block, aphasia, dysphasia, autism and/or other disorders that interfere with how the person is communicating. This card shows up when someone has difficulty understanding someone else’s behaviour. This is the card for miscommunication.

It is difficult moving forward when there is a breakdown in communication. The Eight of Swords can also signal that someone has a hard time dealing with criticism, and that they are internalising the criticism, which ends up interfering with their mental state. The Eight of Swords shows up when we must be more gentle with each other. Use kind words.

SEVEN OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Sneaking off.

2. Avoiding responsibility.

3. Stealing (especially ideas).

4. Breaking the rules.

5. Being caught out.

6. Being afraid to face the music.

7. Dishonesty.

8. Coming and going as you please.

9. Working more efficiently on your own.

Meanings

The Seven of Swords reveals that someone is moving forward in a less than honorable way. There is often dishonesty connected with this card, and trying to get away with something or trying to get out of something by lying. This is the card for thieves. The Seven of Swords combined with the Magician reversed can refer to a con man. The Seven of Swords can be someone who hides the truth, has parasitic energy, comes and goes with or without you knowing about it, or plays around behind your back and don’t pay their way.

The Seven of Swords combined with Three of Swords can be a sign that you are being cheated on, especially if you also have Three of Cups reversed, or combined with other court cards or The High Priestess. The Seven of Swords with Nine of Cups reversed can denote a gambling or drinking problem that are being hidden from you.

Tips for when the Seven of Swords shows up in a reading: there is a narrow-mindedness in this card, and the querent might be unable to see other options and opportunities. Help the querent see other ways of dealing with situations that will be more beneficial for them in the long run.

In a love reading the Seven of Swords can indicate trust issues within a relationship. If the querent is single it can communicate causal relationships with little potential to be anything more.

In career questions the Seven of Swords can denote that you are applying for work in a new company. You are taking the expertise you learned in one company to benefit another one (most likely the competition). This card in a career question can also mean careers in the secret service, espionage, army or marketing.

Personality Types

The Seven of Swords personality is someone who is obnoxious and unavailing, mostly because they are always putting their noses in everybody else’s business. They are dishonest and usually comes and go as they please, living off of others, possibly even stealing from people. They are charming enough that this behavior often goes on for quite some time without anyone becoming suspicious.

A few people see through them though, however a Seven of Swords type person always has an explanation when questioned. There is just something that doesn’t quite make sense and it is hard to put your finger on it. This is someone who will hack into your email and read everything. They will read your diary and look through your phone if they get a chance. Do not trust them.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. A scapegoat.

2. Clumsiness.

3. Forgetfulness.

4. Slander.

Meanings

The Seven of Swords reversed reports clumsiness and forgetfulness, especially when it comes to recollecting which lies have been told and to who. Sneaking around and cheating will not work out, you will lose track of what you are doing. Sometimes the Seven of Swords shows up reversed when the querent is afraid of getting into trouble, even if they haven’t done anything wrong.

They might be a bit paranoid and are aiming at staying under the radar. Sometimes the Seven of Swords shows up reversed when the querent is being used as a scapegoat.

Personality Types

The Seven of Swords reversed exposes someone so messed up they have no idea how to act or how to behave. They are fearful people and do what they can to please others, but all they end up doing is being annoying. This is someone animated and attention seeking. If they are not the center of attention they don’t know what to do with themselves. They interrupt people’s conversations and talk about things they have no knowledge about. Most of the time they feel left out and are desperately trying to get your attention. In personal relationships they drain their partners empty.

They have no hesitation about slandering people if that means being a part of a conversation.

Symbolism

The five Swords in the man’s arms symbolize getting away with something less than honorable. The military camp in the background embodies the rules and laws he is breaking. There are two Swords behind him pointing downwards, representing the apathy of society towards what he has done.

In the background there is a small group of soldiers, one of which has his sword raised, suggesting that he has been caught.

Summary

When the Seven of Swords shows up there might be a fear of both conflict and commitment. The querent might want to get out of anything that can lead to too much responsibility. There is escapism in this card, but not in the same sense as in Seven of Cups (daydreaming). This escapism has more to do with wanting to avoid having to answer to anyone or be held accountable.

Help the querent see how they can be of use without the feeling of being trapped. In relationships the querent might also feel difficulty in expressing themselves freely. When the Seven of Swords represents the querent then the querent prefers being behind the scenes. Being in the public eye is especially difficult when this card shows up.

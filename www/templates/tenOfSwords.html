TEN OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Being stabbed in the back.

2. Being the victim.

3. The end of something.

4. Hitting rock bottom.

5. Feeling useless.

6. The need for a new beginning.

7. The end of darkness. Only way forward is up.

8. Unable to cope with demands.

Meanings

Ten of Swords is the card for endings and loss, backstabbing and lack of support. Sometimes this card represents hitting rock bottom. It has the mentality of "when it rains, it pours", and the querent might be feeling like there is no end to the suffering. However on a more positive note, this card often shows up when there is an opportunity to end the suffering. The worst has happened and you survived it. On a day-to-day basis the Ten of Swords can mean feeling depleted and tired, unable to cope with the demands.

In a love reading the Ten of Swords reflects a need for renewal in relationships. If the querent is single, then it can signal the end of being single.

In a career reading the Ten of Swords signifies work that involves death, such as a funeral director, detective, or the field of forensic science. It can also mean the querent is ready to retire. The Ten of Swords can also mean unemployment, especially if it shows up reversed.

Personality Types

The Ten of Swords personality is someone who often finds themselves in hardship and struggle. They want to free themselves from dependency and feel relieved when their relationships, jobs and projects end. They often end dramatically and even violently, but it still gives the person a sense of freedom. This is someone who struggles with being happy.

They feel comfortable with gloom, where they can lick their wounds. They know they need to heal, and dream about better times, but are afraid to actually live a new way of life. They find all kinds of relationships exhausting, mostly because of their constant feelings of failure and ruin. They come across as cold and distant.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Holding on to pain.

2. Being a scapegoat.

3. Mental torture.

Meanings

While the Ten of Swords is reversed it signifies holding on to pain, often pain inflicted by others. This querent might not be able to let go of what other people have inflicted on them, and is feeling no support. Not just that there is no support, but that there might even be deliberate sabotage.

This card often shows up reversed when the querent is being lured into a deliberate plot, in which they become the scapegoat and have to take the blame for other people’s misconduct. Other times this card shows up reversed when the querent is so tired and worn down that they make a mistake that will cost them dearly.

Personality Types

The Ten of Swords reversed portrays someone so scared of failing that they live in constant terror and anger. Their reputation is often poor as a result. They play mind games as a way to salvage their reputation. They often promise the world and deliver little, if anything. They leave their family and friends constantly disappointed. They need to take a step back and face their fears, or at least admit that they have them.

The Ten of Swords reversed indicates someone who is unable to admit short comings. They need to be perfect and find themselves exhausted as a result. They lack the inner substance to see what is really important. Their lives look good on the surface, but is in reality a very shallow and empty existence with few good memories.

Symbolism

The man laying on the ground with the ten swords in his back embodies hitting rock bottom. The ten swords in asymmetrical order represent defeat, and endings that are completely unmanageable to the man on the ground. The red cape stands for his flesh.

The lower grouping of six swords in it connote having nowhere to run and nowhere to hide. The four swords in the man’s upper body illustrate unwanted endings and loss. The sun is rising in the background dispelling the darkness, hinting at a new beginning.

Summary

When the Ten of Swords shows up in your cards it denotes a time during which you are faced with endings of some sorts. You might even feel grief and sorrow. This is a time when you must face up to change.

The Ten of Swords also comes about when the querent has invested themselves into a way of thinking which turns out to longer serve them. Their beliefs might have been proven wrong or they realize they can no longer go on the way they used to. There has to be a renewal. You either renew yourself or face peril, sink or swim. Things can no longer stay the same.

This card is present when the querent needs to renew themselves, and realize that the world still needs them. One way or the other life will get interesting again. It doesn’t have to be through work. It can be anything the querent wants it to be.

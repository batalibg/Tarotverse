QUEEN OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Cynical and cold. Impractical high ideals.

2. Intelligent and clear in her dealings.

3. Witty and smart.

4. Professional.

5. Career oriented.

6. Often hurt in love.

7. Well organised.

8. Perfectionist.

Meanings

The Queen of Swords expresses a person who is cold, professional and smart. The Queen of Swords is witty and funny in a sophisticated way. She often represents single people who have been hurt in love, and are bitter and hurt, but good at covering it up. This card is career oriented. It is intellectually oriented, rather than feeling oriented.

The Queen of Swords rules the world of intellect, and this card can denote manager positions, or councilor/advisory positions. This card describes an emotionally cold but well organized (and sometimes controlling) environment.

Staying professional is a must. You might feel you are under scrutiny and all eyes are on you. Follow the rules, because there is not much room for artistic interpretations when the Queen of Swords describes a situation.

In a love reading this card signifies difficulties in letting the wall come down. In a relationship reading is can indicate the querent is their own worst enemy.

In a career reading the Queen of Swords signifies intellectual work: lecturer, professor, manager, or coordinator.

Personality Types

The Queen of Swords portrays someone mature, intelligent and independent. There is something classy about Queen of Swords people, and others are instinctively at their best behaviour around them. These sorts of people tolerate no non-sense. Queen of Swords type people are sometimes more interested in finding out if other people are doing the right thing, rather than if they are themselves doing what is right.

This is someone who puts people on the spot, and makes theym feel like they are under scrutiny. This personality type needs to be at the top end of society and in prestigious organisations in order to feel comfortable. They are often very educated. They believe in social justice and fight honourable causes (without getting their own hands dirty).

They excel at organisational activities in which they can oversee the work, rather than being hands on. They are very convincing people and do well in politics. They have a brilliant sense of humour and are irresistible once they turn on their charm. This is someone who has acquired broad and varied life experiences. They are courageous, cool, calm and collected. They also know they are smarter than most people.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Ruthless.

2. Lack of compassion.

3. Problems with phones, emails. Language barrier.

Meanings

While the Queen of Swords is reversed it indicates issues with problem solving and communication. It can denote accusations being made without facts being checked. There are also problems with reading emails and answering phone calls.

These problems can be exterior, like problems with computers and phones, as well as language barriers. Sometimes this card shows up reversed when the querent feels misunderstood, or are misunderstanding everyone else. The querent might not feel very connected and is overly emotional as a result.

The Queen of Swords often shows up reversed when the querent needs to be more forgiving. It can also signify someone who is going through a bitter divorce.

Personality Types

The Queen of Swords reversed describes someone with a ruthless tendency. They have little to no compassion for others. They often jump down other peoples throats, accusing them of all sorts of things, without the facts to back up their claims. They rarely investigate facts and are always jumping to conclusions. They are cold-hearted and harsh.

They know how to give you a convincing smile, however their cold eyes will always give them away. This is someone who needs to figure out what they want and take appropriate actions. They need to get out of their heads and connect to their hearts. This is someone who hasn’t felt anything emotional in a very long time.

Symbolism

The clouds connote mental challenges. The woman’s head is above the clouds, exhibiting her intellect rising above these challenges. Her sword is in her right hand pointing upwards, expressing her clarity. The bird above her head reflects the mind’s ability to find solutions to problems. Her left hand extended represents her ability to offer clear advice. Her throne is decorated with an image of a winged child, a pair of moons and butterflies.

The winged child signals blessings, the moons refers to intuition and the butterflies symbolize transformation. Her butterfly crown suggests her intellect’s ability to adapt to any situation. Her cloak decorated in clouds insinuates changes. The red tassels on her sleeves means passion. Her white gown bespeaks purity.

Summary

The Queen of Swords is the most demanding of all the court cards, even more so than King of Swords. This card can often be unreasonable, and people many find themselves tip toeing around the Queen of Swords in fear of setting her off. It can represent either a male/female, or a adult/child. The energy of the Queen of Swords makes others feel uneasy.

The Queen of Swords can often rely on others too much, and can become a parasitic energy in your life. It can take over your life in a way you might not even notice at first. Her words are harsh. Don’t take it personally. Of all the Queens this card is the most damaged, especially if she shows up reversed.

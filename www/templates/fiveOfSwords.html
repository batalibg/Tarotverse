FIVE OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Conflict and defeat.

2. Winning at all costs attitude.

3. Tension and betrayal.

4. Unhealthy competition.

5. Personal attacks.

6. Being lied to.

7. Bullying. Being harmed by others.

8. Being in trouble without doing anything wrong.

Meanings

The Five of Swords expresses defeat and betrayal, conflict or unhealthy competition. Words will be hurtful, lies will be told and your weaknesses may be used against you. If this card shows up with the Justice card reversed someone might try to get you into trouble by lying, or making false accusations.

If this card shows up in a love reading, someone is not quite what they seem. It also might mean you're dealing with obnoxious people or situations.

In a love reading the Five of Swords signals betrayal and abusive relationships. If the querent is single it can reflect dating the wrong people.

In a career reading the Five of Swords signifies unhappiness in career. It can also mean work involving damage control, internal investigations, police or army careers.

Personality Types

The Five of Swords indicates someone who can be a bully, or quarrelsome. This is someone very competitive who wants to win at all costs, especially if it means making others fail. They love getting people into trouble and they go out of their way to create drama and conflict. Things get very uncomfortable when Five of Swords type people are around. They live by the sword and die by the sword.

They believe if they don’t get the advantage over others, others will get the advantage over them. They long for what they can not have, and their dreams turn into delusions as their reality is often toxic due to their behaviour. This is someone who is very good at pretending to be your friend, but who will find out your weaknesses and use it against you.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Unresolved conflicts.

2. Fighting about nothing.

3. Inability to defend oneself.

Meanings

The Five of Swords reversed denotes conflict and arguments remaining unresolved, often dragging on indefinitely. People might be nice to your face, but are not behind your back. There is no resolution to conflicts, as there are no winners. The reasons behind the arguments were lost a long time ago, and people fight about nothing.

Tip to the reader: The Five of Swords often shows up reversed when the querent does everything in their power to avoid conflict, as they are certain they will lose. If someone picks on them they timidly backs off. Help your querent find healthy ways to stand up for themselves.

Personality Types

The Five of Swords reversed indicates someone who has internal conflict and inner anger, but with too much fear to openly express how they feel. They have a victim mentality, and are convinced everyone is against them. This is someone who feels defeated and hopeless due to their lack of self realization. Their dreams seem distant and there is no happiness in day-to-day life.

They come across as spineless and weak, and few people respect them. They often hide behind much stronger people who take advantage of them in return for protection. On their own they are unable to handle themselves very well and are only considered someone when in a group context. On their own they feel worthless and useless.

Symbolism

The irregular shaped clouds symbolise a time of conflict. The two Swords on the ground represent the defeat of the two people walking away. The two swords pointing upwards in the man’s left hand infer the man’s competitive nature. The sword in his right hand is pointing downwards and signifies the imbalance between the conscious and the subconscious that leads to tension and betrayal.

Summary

The Five of Swords shows up in your reading when the querent is unable to get what they want and need. Help your client to see other options as they most likely feel stuck and helpless. Be careful who you trust when the Five of Swords shows up. Look at the other cards to see where the conflicts stem from.

The best way to deal with the Five of Swords energy is to detach from all drama. Once you become uninterested in other people's drama they will simply fade away. The situation will then start to improve as a result.

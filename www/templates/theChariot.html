THE CHARIOT

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Victory and success.

2. Being a hero.

3. Using your will power to accomplish your goals.

4. Being assertive.

5. Being in control.

6. Getting your way.

7. Looking out for your own interests.

8. Travelling.

9. Your vehicle.

Meanings

The Chariot card represents your will-power and determination. It represents victory through assertiveness. The Chariot gives you the green light to charge ahead and take control over your life. Your success depends on your will and ability to be in control, and to be brave enough to move forward even in unknown territory. In a reading the Chariot reminds us to steer our lives in the direction we desire. If we apply will-power and motivation, we can create our own destiny. Set yourself goals. The Chariot will speed up the process and get you there quicker. The Chariot can also represent travel, or your vehicle.

In a love reading the Chariot denotes the importance of loving yourself first.

In a career reading the Chariot signifies careers in transport, quality control, software, or audits.

Personality Types

Chariot type people are very strong-willed. They are determined to succeed in this world and come across as fearless. Sometimes they fake it until they make it, and they are rarely as confident as they seem. What drives them is their desire to do well, and they work hard to get what they want. This is someone who puts in the effort. They can be diligent, self-composed, self-aware, controlling and brave.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Lack of direction.

2. Delay.

3. Self-Doubt.

Meanings

The Chariot card reversed indicates defeat and cowardice. Instead of charging ahead there is the feeling of giving up before even trying. "What is the use?" is the energy when the Chariot is reversed. The querent is too self-conscious to move forward. The outside environment is not very favourable either. On a more practical note, the Chariot shows up sometimes reversed when the querent is experiencing car trouble or becomes stagnant due to loss of vehicle.

Personality Types

The Chariot reversed is someone with low self-esteem, and little control over their emotions. They lack direction in their lives and are afraid of success. Sometimes they are interested in so many things that they get lost and fail to choose a path for themselves. There is often sadness within and even overwhelming grief. This is someone who might have fatigue and social anxiety. They struggle with reality, and are moody or overly sensitive. Rather than taking responsibility over their situation they complain and end up playing the victim role. To get things done they need to be pressured by outside forces, like an angry boss. They are motivated by a fear of failure rather than a hunger for success.

Astrology

The Chariot is ruled by the sign Cancer, which is ruled by the Moon. The crab has a hard shell, symbolizing the need to be in control over your emotions. With the crab comes claws that tend to grab things to hang on to, especially the home, family, friends and possessions. Cancer is the first water sign and deals primarily with the ebb and flow of life. Despite any obstacles that may come our way we must proceed forward towards what will ultimately enlighten us.

Symbolism

The eight pointed star crown represents the octagram of creation. The union of lingam and yoni symbolise inseparability of the male and female principles and the totality of creation. The crescent moon on the hero’s shoulder promises new beginnings. The winged sun is the solar disc of Horus, a symbol of protection.

His chariot is the cubic stone, illustrating stability, and perfection of work through effort (from rough stone to a perfect cubic stone, the unshakable foundation of any building). The hero is the spirit emerging from the stone, representing purification of spirit through earthly efforts. The six pointed stars on his canopy represent the Seal of Solomon and its mystical properties. The eight pointed star on the hero’s crown stand for balance, harmony and cosmic order.

On the hero’s chest is a square suggesting the fourfold world. On the shield is a tantric sexual emblem indicating that the vehicle is in fact the Hero’s drive and energy. The black and white sphinxes represent inner victory over the lower forces of its own nature through a journey of purification.

KNIGHT OF CUPS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. A declaration of love. Being in love with love.

2. A business trip.

3. A singer, poet, painter or writer.

4. A creative phase.

5. Wearing rose-colored glasses.

6. Optimism.

7. Putting on a performance. Entertaining others.

Meanings

The Knight of Cups conveys a person on a quest to declare his love. This is your knight in shining armor. They are a singer, poet and writer. They paint and create, wherever they go. They are charming and love romance. In most situations Knight of Cups often speaks to a creative phase in your life, where music and art will play a huge part. The Knight of Cups often shows up in your cards when you are travelling, when the act of travel feels joyful and meaningful. Your imagination will be at a peak, especially when combined with the Moon card.

In a love reading the Knight of Cups often shows up when someone takes you on a romantic getaway. If single, it often represents a love interest. In the context of a relationship the Knight of Cups can mean the couple is moving forward in a very positive way, and it often includes physical travel as well.

In a career reading the Knight of Cups signifies a poet, musician, painter, or any type of creative jobs. This card can also mean health care jobs, but only if the querent really loves to care for others. The Knight of Cups can also signify a business trip.

Personality Types

The Knight of Cups is someone who is in love with love, and they are often wearing rose-colored glasses. They are dramatic, romantic and optimistic, and they have strong emotional responses. They are very artistically and musically inclined.

This is someone who is bringing emotions to the surface, both in themselves and in others. These people are very charming, loving, adaptable and they are easily influenced by their environment and the people around them.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Cynicism.

2. Pretending.

3. Leaving someone or something behind.

Meanings

When the Knight of Cups is reversed it alludes to a person who is walking away from a relationship and/or a creative venture. The emotional state of the querent might be far from romantic, instead it is more likely to be cynical and they use their insights and intuition to make others hurt as much as they do. Lazy and untruthful, this person is someone who might even hate the opposite sex. Knight of Cups reversed indicates intimacy issues.

Personality Types

If the Knight of Cups is reversed it marks someone who is putting on a performance rather than being themselves. The performance is draining and they end up exhausted. They are so emotional that there is no rest from their current circumstance.

They have no idea who they are or who the persona is they created to keep everyone else happy and entertained. These sorts of people are excellent actors/actresses. They attract people like a moth to a flame, but they have deep-rooted intimacy issues, and will not let anyone close.

Symbolism

The white horse symbolises the bearer of heroes. The knight acts as a messenger, often bearing a romantic message. The cloak is decorated with images of fish, which stands for the spirit. The wings on the helmet and feet are a symbol of freedom of expression. The river intimates the flow of emotions.

Summary

The Knight of Cups hints that you are moving in the right direction, and things are speeding up. You might feel unprepared for what is expected of you, and you might even feel like you have to fake it before you make it. The best thing you can do is to be yourself, because you are attracting attention with your charisma and personality. If you pretend to be someone else it will only decrease your natural light that people are so drawn to. You are a sensitive and beautiful soul, and people feel they can relate to you. The Knight of Cups often shows up in your cards when you are about to go on a significant physical journey.

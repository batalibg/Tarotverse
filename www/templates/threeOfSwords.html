THREE OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Cutting to the heart of a situation.

2. Heart break.

3. Sorrow.

4. Separation.

5. Emotional pain.

6. Being let down or letting someone down (including yourself).

7. Afraid of getting hurt.

8. Finding it hard to trust.

9. Being betrayed by someone in a trusted position.

Meanings

The Three of Swords infers heartbreak, sorrow, pain and separation. Sometimes the separation is only temporary, other times it is final. The other cards in the spread will tell you in what situation this pain is manifesting itself, and how strongly it is affecting you. This card will also show old pain that has been covered up, which needs help releasing. If surrounded by a lot of court cards your heart is hurting due to the people in your life.

The Three of Swords combined with the Five of Swords can mean that someone is deliberately trying to hurt you. Don't let them. The Three of Swords often shows up to show you how to get to the heart of a situation. It also sometimes shows up for people with a love of music, especially string music.

Tips for when Three of Swords shows up in a reading: remind the querent of their passions and their dreams. Get them motivated about life again. Steer them away from wanting to escape their problems. They need to get themselves back on track first.

In a love reading the Three of Swords denotes a painful time where the querent is dealing with rejection or separation.

In a career reading it signifies work that puts the querent under stress. It can also refer to a cardiologist, surgeon or grief counselor.

Personality Types

The Three of Swords characterizes someone who is easily offended and hurt due to past abandonment and loss. They are terrified of being rejected and are controlling or jealous people. These people don't know what it feels like to be secure, and often have twisted beliefs about love. As a result they never really give their heart to anyone, and can be very difficult people to love.

Their partners try to fix them and counsel them, but it usually ends with more heartache. They are often stuck in the past and are reliving old trauma. They seek relationships to feel whole, but as their relationship develops they suffer anxiety and a fear of loss. As a consequence they are often in and out of relationships, and they never seem to settle down.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Deep emotional pain.

2. Abuse.

3. Neglect.

Meanings

The Three of Swords reversed marks that there is blocked grief stored in the person's heart. The querent might be afraid of grieving, and is internalizing their pain. This card often shows up when people don't tell others how they are really feeling.

They have problems putting up the correct boundaries, and become punching bags for other peoples aggression and frustrations. They keep taking the abuse, believing they have no other choice. They don't honor their own feelings. This card also shows up when people reject others in fear of being rejected themselves.

Personality Types

If the Three of Swords is reversed bespeaks someone who is calm on the surface, but is hiding something disturbing underneath. They often seem confident, and hide behind a fake smile. There is frequently deep-seated issues with abuse, neglect and loss that have never been processed. The person has never given themselves an opportunity to heal.

They know how to act as if nothing is wrong. It seems like nothing upsets or worries them, but only because they have learned to hide their feelings. Somewhere along the way these people suffered greatly, and they have learned to control their outwardly responses. What you see is not what you get.

Symbolism

The three swords pointing downwards symbolize endings. The swords cross each other as they pierce a heart, expressing emotional pain. The sky is filled with rainy clouds, illustrating grief.

Summary

The Three of Swords is the start of the more negative influences of the Swords. The Swords in tarot are about conflict. Sometimes conflict is necessary. When combined with Three, which is about communication, it can denote breakdown in communication. The Three of Swords, on a more positive note, is about cutting to the heart of an issue to resolve it. On a more negative side, it is about hurt feelings and criticism.

Most people would rather be ruined by flattery than saved by criticism. We all have to face criticism from time to time. It is how we handle it, and learn or grow from it that matters. The Three of Swords meaning can mean that the querent is learning not to take everything so personally.

TEN OF WANDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Being burdened with work and responsibilities.

2. Being a martyr.

3. Unable to say No.

4. Being task oriented.

5. Worrying what other people think of you.

6. Performance punishment.

7. Making other people happy instead of themselves.

8. Waiting for their big break.

Meanings

The Ten of Wands is the card for hard work and taking on more responsibility. Don’t lose sight of the bigger picture, remember why you are putting in all the effort. Sometimes the querent is feeling burned out and weighed down when this card is present.

They only see the wood for trees. The Ten of Wands often shows up when the querent is unable to say no, and people are taking advantage of them.

In a love reading the Ten of Wands can signify a high-maintenance relationship, or that the querent is putting work first.

In a career reading Ten of Wands means working on too many projects and suffering performance anxiety.

Personality Types

The Ten of Wands persona is someone with a creative side who is very talented, but who is also suffering performance anxiety. They are so dependable in both their professional and personal lives that they end up carrying all the responsibilities. They are often weighed down and worn out. They believe if they just keep on going that maybe someone will see how much they are doing and come and help out.

That unfortunately rarely happens. These people are the ones that will help everyone else, but rarely receive any help themselves. They often blame themselves unnecessarily, and guilt keeps them trapped in work and worry. These characters are often people pleasers to their own detriment.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Buckle under pressure.

2. Throwing it all in.

3. Avoiding responsibility.

Meanings

The Ten of Wands reversed shows fear of responsibilities, and an inability to keep on top of things. The querent might be unmotivated and ready to walk away from their duties. This card often shows up when the querent has lost motivation to do hard work. They might not see the meaning of it anymore and want to invest their energies elsewhere.

Personality Types

When the Ten of Wands is reversed it denotes someone who is afraid to use their talents for fear that they will have to take on more responsibility. They are so used to be the one pointing the finger and playing the blame game that if they were the ones making decisions they know it would all come back to them. They are afraid of being judged and hide behind others: unions, policies and so forth, so that no one can target them.

They twist situations and words to make it sound like they are the victims. They have trapped themselves in their own fears of failure and are unable to free themselves. They often end up running away from opportunities that could have taken them far. They are unwilling to grow and are instead going in so many directions that they really are not going anywhere. They are usually in a state of feeling overwhelmed. Deep down they feel hopelessness.

Symbolism

The Wands being carried are responsibilities. They are blocking his view, representing task orientation rather than seeing the bigger picture. He is looking at the earth, asserting that he is concerned about logistics. His orange tunic demonstrates determination. His red belt shows his drive to finish what he started.

His brown shoes illustrate his practicality. He is moving towards a building in the background. The building reflects the security that he wants to achieve and maintain.

Summary

The Ten of Wands can mean the querent is hiding behind a role, a work commitment, or a uniform so that they don’t have to ask what it is they really want to do. This card shows up when it is time to take steps towards your dreams. The ten wands are currently blinding you, and you might have lost track of the bigger picture. Remember back to when you were a child - what was it you really wanted to do?

You might have to do a complete U-turn, or you might be on your way. It might even be what you are currently doing has taken over too much of your life. You need to let people help you and support you. No man is an island.

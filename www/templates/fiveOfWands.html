FIVE OF WANDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Arguments and bickering.

2. Being irritated.

3. Wanting to stand out from the crowd.

4. Clashing of personalities.

5. Opposition.

6. Doing sport.

7. Being annoyed by demands.

Meanings

The Five of Wands suggests standing up for what is important to you, even if it means meeting some opposition. It can speak to arguments or quarrels. Everyone involved wants to get their opinions across, and this creates tension. The Five of Wands can also mean people have personality clashes, or that they find themselves easily irritated by others.

In a love reading the Five of Wands signals arguments between couples.

In a career reading Five of Wands signifies competitive jobs, sporting careers and physical work.

Personality Types

the Five of Wands indicates someone who has problems with authority figures and experts. They doubt themselves and cover up their insecurities by hiding behind a cold and prideful exterior. This is someone who is fighting their own insecurities, and are often in fight mode. As a result they suffer exhaustion, and get overwhelmed or stressed by the smallest of changes.

They need to be more patient with themselves and others. They have a tendency to overreact and make things out to be a lot worse than what it is. This is someone who is fighting a battle with themselves and is therefore fighting the whole world.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Avoiding conflict.

2. Self-doubt.

3. Loss of individuality.

Meanings

The Five of Wands reversed indicates loss of individuality. There is a struggle to stand out among others who are equally talented. The querent might have little faith in winning and therefore don’t even try. This card also shows up reversed when there is a fear of conflict or a fear of success. The querent might be overly defensive, believing everyone is attacking them.

Personality Types

The Five of Wands reversed signals someone who needs special love and attention. They need to know that they matter, and that they are special. They often get ignored as they tend to have low self-esteem and issues with speaking up for themselves. They are easily over-powered, which feeds their low self-image. They often give up easily, believing they are failures. They want to be an individual, but are afraid to stand out in case they get attacked.

Tip to the reader: the Five of Wands reversed can communicate that the querent needs to ‘find themselves’. They need to do some soul-searching and find out who they are. Once they are in touch with their essential character their life will start flowing in amazing ways.

Symbolism

The five wands have the potential to form a pentacle if the people work together. The wands clash together, symbolising conflict and competition. The people are only using their wands to strike against the others, rather than each other, showing that no one is getting seriously hurt.

The uneven ground illustrates difficulty in moving forward. The five people are all wearing different coloured clothes, showing there is no team, and that each man is their own.

Summary

There is a time to follow and there is a time to lead. The Five of Wands speaks of a time where you are somewhere in between. You are not sure if you are ready to lead, but you are not trusting in who to follow either. The result is often bickering and petty conflicts. Someone will have to take the lead, even if it means sticking your neck out.

If you don’t like the leader, and you don’t want to be the leader, you might end up a loner. Find out who you are and what you believe in, and your life will become much easier. You are not meant to follow everyone, however you are not meant to fight everyone either. Finding out who you are and believing in yourself will make you less argumentative and more able to stand on your own two feet.

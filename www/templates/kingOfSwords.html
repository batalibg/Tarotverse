KING OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Authority and responsibility.

2. Power and ambition.

3. A professional man.

4. Well educated.

5. Being well prepared.

6. Having a good reputation.

7. Being polite in public, but can be rude to people they know.

8. Cold and aloof.

Meanings

The King of Swords portrays a professional who is at the top of their game. This is someone who is an expert in their field, and studied to acquire this knowledge. They also have plenty of practical experience in how to apply this knowledge in a very sophisticated manner. The King of Swords is an intellectual authority figure who are often chefs, lawyers, judges, doctors, surgeons and pilots.

In interpersonal situations the King of Swords signifies success, especially if you prepared. The more prepared you are the better the outcome will be. This is a unemotional card, so it is better to know your stuff then to try to rely on charm and luck to get through.

The King of Swords in a love reading can denote a committed relationship with someone who is very independent.

In a career reading the King of Swords signifies being a master at a certain skill, and being in high demand. It can also express authority and can be a policeman, judge or lawyer.

Personality Types

The King of Swords denotes a rational and ethical person who is often mentoring others who need to learn from their expertise. The King of Swords personalities are our experts, but different form a know-it-all, because they actually do know. They have no need to flaunt their knowledge, they are already highly regarded people. They are successful and very confident.

They stay with the facts and rarely makes things personal. Their reputation is of uppermost importance to them. They are very well-educated, and have well-paid and well-respected positions in society. This is someone who makes good decisions and who usually win an argument.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Cruelty.

2. Censorship.

3. Holding back truths.

4. Injustice.

Meanings

When the King of Swords is reversed it indicates someone who will manipulate the facts to get their own ways. There are blocks in regards to integrity and objectivity. This is someone opinionated and biased. New ideas get mocked and censored. It sometimes shows up reversed when the querent is holding back truths they should be telling.

There is ignorance to this card when it shows up reversed, and the person might even be tyrannical and abusive, especially verbally. In situations wherein the King of Swords is reversed it can mean the querent is unprepared and unable to accomplish tasks. When reversed in a love reading it can signal an abusive relationship.

Personality Types

The King of Swords reversed type personality are people with a nasty and hateful nature. They want to disgrace others as much as possible, especially through humiliation. Deep down they are defeated and unable to handle their own emotional reactions. They demand respect, but do nothing to actually deserve any. This is someone who is bad-tempered and hostile.

They are emotionally unavailable and care only about themselves. If you cross them they will never forget it. When they start awakening to love and light, it is a very slow process, and once you think they finally have got it, they revert back to their usual cruel and arrogant selves.

Symbolism

The ring on the king’s left Saturn finger symbolizes authority and responsibility. His blue tunic indicates knowledge and understanding. His purple cape reflects power and ambition. The clouds represent mental challenges.

The two birds flying above the clouds represent Hugin and Munin, inferring information available to the king at all times. The double edged sword in the king’s right hand relates the king’s power see both sides of a situation. His throne is decorated with symbols of butterflies, two crescent moons and an angel.

The butterflies represent transformation. The two crescent moons are the balance between male and female energies. The angel next to his left ear implies guidance. The trees symbolizes education.

Summary

The King of Swords expresses mastery and expertise. If you have become complacent in your role it arrives to get you up to speed again. The King of Swords can be an authority you feel intimidated by, however if you put your pride aside, the King of Swords will be exactly what you need, especially in career questions. Te King of Swords is demanding, but if you listen to it'll make a lot of sense.

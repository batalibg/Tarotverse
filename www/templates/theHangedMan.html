THE HANGED MAN

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Letting go of control.

2. Seeing life from a new perspective.

3. Letting go of your position to get peace.

4. Pausing to reflect.

5. Appreciation.

6. Learning from mistakes.

7. Sacrificing your self-interest to benefit a group.

8. Putting others first.

9. Waiting for a better opportunity.

Meanings

The Hanged Man represents being temporarily suspended. Life is on hold, but it serves a purpose. While you are being suspended you gain a whole new perspective, and this perspective is necessary for you to gain the insight and enlightenment necessary to take you to your next level. If you were to get hanged, what thoughts would flood your mind. The Hanged Man speaks of the kind of clarity you have just before you are about to lose it all. You would suddenly know what was of true importance, how much you love the people in your life, and even love for life itself.

The Hanged Man tells you to reflect upon what is truly important to you, without having to have a near death experience. The Hanged Man does not get grounded and balanced through putting his feet on the ground; instead he achieves it through putting his head towards the ground where he starts to listen more.

In a love reading The Hanged Man signifies taking a step back in your relationship. You will achieve much more by doing a lot less. If you are very invested in your relationship The Hanged Man says you must now invest in yourself as an individual. Your partner will start noticing you when you stop trying to get his/hers attention. If the querent is single The Hanged Man signifies manifesting love by learning to love life as single. The Hanged Man achieves his through appreciation.

In a career reading The Hanged Man signifies careers in pharmaceuticals, psychology and innovation.

Personality Types

The Hanged Man speaks of someone who is sacrificing parts of themselves to create a higher good. They don’t like conflict and will often carry most of the load to keep others happy and balanced. They are either doing too much or too little to help themselves. They like to escape into daydreams and need to pause regularly for reflection. When feeling attacked they often fall apart emotionally and can lash out dramatically. Their ideas are often unconventional and they both feel different and are different from the rest of us.

The Hanged Man is viewing the world from a different perspective and is trying to figure out how the world works through observation. This is someone who is a mystic and a visionary, and who is abiding their time to make their move. This person might very well be a genius, something that is rarely appreciated in their time, due to their own inconstant and vague efforts.

The Hanged Man is looking for intellectual connections with other people, rather than physical ones. He feels whole and complete already. He is not really hungry for more, he believes he already has everything he needs. He wants to invent and create, and he doesn’t need any recognition for his efforts as he already feels blessed. He loves nature and outdoor activities.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Egotism.

2. Inability to change.

3. Missing an opportunity.

Meanings

The Hanged Man reversed exhibits stubbornness and selfishness, refusing to give up your perspective to gain new insight. This card reversed shows someone who is feeling away from the world, and is unable to communicate their point of view or perspective to others. Often with this card reversed there is no sacrifice and no new insight. As a consequence the querent is unable to accomplish what they wanted. The querent is not willing to suffer the pain, so there is no gain either.

Personality Types

The Hanged Man reversed is someone with strange antisocial behaviour. They might even show sociopathic or even psychopathic tendencies. The Hanged Man reversed conveys a person lost in their own ideas and beliefs, often with a poor or non-existent grip on reality. They might be completely living in a dream, deceiving themselves and others for their own selfish purposes. They might even sacrifice themselves for a cause that is false or in the belief that it will help someone else when in reality it will not. This is someone with a personality disorder, mental illness or a lack of moral.

Astrology

The Hanged Man is ruled by Neptune and connected to the water element. Neptune rules drugs of any sort, both legal and illegal. Additionally Neptune indicates fogs, gasses and sleep disorders, hypnosis and asphyxiation. Well placed, Neptune indicates psychics and idealists, writers, magicians, artists and musicians. It is the planet for inspiration. Neptune purifies and asks us to sacrifice for the higher good, or for the love we have for one another. Neptune rules visual communication such as photography, film, dance and paintings. When poorly placed, Neptune has issues with reality and suggests the querent might be missing something or even hiding something. There might be memory problems, insanity, delusions and nervous breakdowns. Neptune also rules psychopaths, antisocial disorder and sociopath. Neptune also rules the sea and is connected to the water element. Psychologically, Neptune makes us want to escape reality and to enter into a heavenly, blissful state. If poorly aspected this can make us vague, inconsistent and confused.

Symbolism

The image of the Hanged Man recalls the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn, an organisation devoted to the study and practice of the occult. The gallows from which the man is suspended symbolizes the world tree, connoting there is learning and wisdom to be gained when this card shows up.

The position of his legs forms a Fylfot cross which indicates the direction of the sun. The halo around his head marks a holy person or spiritual attainment. It is also a reference to the sun, inferring enlightenment. His red pants implies passion. His blue coat communicates knowledge. His yellow shoes represents idealism.

The man’s hands folded at the back of his body illustrate a time for non-action. His suspended state exhibits life being on hold, a waiting period. It also suggests seeing the world or a situation from a different angle. The man has lost his position, but his face says he did so willingly, to further his goal. The peaceful expression on his face alludes to his free will.

Summary

The Hanged Man means doing less achieves more. The more you let go, the more things will start to flow. The Hanged Man is willing to sacrifice being right in order to have peace in his life. When the Hanged Man shows up in your spread you will have to chose: You can be right or you can have peace.

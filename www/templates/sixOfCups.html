SIX OF CUPS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Your childhood.

2. Memories.

3. Pleasures.

4. Innocence.

5. Your past returning to you.

6. Your home is where your heart is.

7. Feeling at home.

8. Being childlike.

9. Good will.

10. Being kind to someone.

Meanings

The Six of Cups is the card for sensuality and pleasure, memories, innocence, nostalgia and childhood. Sometimes it shows up when someone from the past is on their way back into your life again. The Six of Cups recalls a worry free and happy childhood. It speaks of children and your inner child. It reminds us that our home is where our hearts are. The Six of Cups often shows up when the querent feels at home where they are and with what they are doing.

In a love reading the Six of Cups can relate a sensual relationship that brings joy to the querent. It can mean old love returning, or it can mean love that connects you to your inner child. The Six of Cups denotes a kind relationship where the couple is free to be themselves. In a career reading the Six of Cups signifies feeling at home with work, and feeling safe at work with the people you work alongside.

The work environment is very favourable when the Six of Cups shows up. It can also mean careers in childcare and hospitality. This card can also suggests work that involves delving into the past, such as a historian or investigator.

Personality Types

The Six of Cups portrays someone very sensual, who must learn to respect their own and others boundaries. These people have a love for pleasure and sensual excess, with a roller coaster response to the highs and lows of their own emotions. They have a tendency to be stuck in the nostalgia of the past and they often have a childlike nature. They often have deep-rooted issues that need to be addressed.

They escape dealing with their emotions by seeking pleasures and excessive sensual experiences. They tend to live too much on the physical plane and need to have a look at why they get so stuck in their past. They seem like the happiest people on the planet, but there are often tears behind their smile. The Six of Cups type people are often the kindest people you will ever meet.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Bad memories.

2. Feeling out of place.

3. Memory loss.

Meanings

Six of Cups reversed indicates a difficult childhood and haunting, even disturbing childhood memories. Memories of all kinds can be an issue when this card shows up reversed, even memory loss. It can also indicate difficulties in growing and evolving. Six of Cups often shows up reversed when the querent is trying to make others happy which is ultimately doomed to fail. The querent has to make themselves happy first.

Personality Types

The Six of Cups reversed bespeaks someone with a tendency to quit before they’ve finished what they started. They often lean towards extreme lifestyles that can be dangerous and uncomfortable. They are seeking pleasure and thrills regardless of the long-term consequences.

They are usually full of contradictions, and they have no healthy boundaries in their life. They’re escaping traumatic childhood memories with unhealthy lifestyle habits and addictions. They don’t know who they are, and often imitate the personalities of the people they are with.

Symbolism

The five petal flowers symbolize innocence, wonder and purity. The children represent the past and memories. The taller child is handing a cup with a flower to the younger child, illustrating kindness. The older person in the background is walking away, signaling that the future is now. The harvested garden implies abundance. The house stands for security.

Summary

Home is where the heart is, and when you follow your heart you might end up in some interesting places. Six of Cups is a card that makes you feel right at home in your environment and there is often a link to childhood emotions of magic. There is a purity to this card that is difficult to describe, but once you are under its spell life is both exciting and safe in a wonderful blend.

SEVEN OF WANDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Being in a advantageous position.

2. Being a chameleon.

3. Being defensive.

4. Putting up boundaries.

5. Being a teacher or public speaker.

6. Being overwhelmed by too much attention.

7. Getting what you want, and then not wanting it anymore.

Meanings

The Seven of Wands conveys being defensive and putting up barriers and boundaries. You are protecting your point of view and your position. You have the advantageous position. People might find it hard to get close to you when this card shows up. In a business question you might have an overly narrow niche of customers you want to serve, and you might have to broaden your customer platform.

This card can also be an indication of how you handle being in demand, as the person on the picture is a bit overwhelmed by the attention he is getting. The Seven of Wands also shows up when the querent is hearing the call to teach.

In a love reading the Seven of Wands often means the querent is afraid to get hurt, and is putting up walls.

In a career question it signifies careers in teaching, politics or management.

Personality Types

The Seven of Wands character is someone who stands up for what they believe in, and their opinions often put them in the minority. They have sound knowledge to back up their beliefs, and they fight ignorance in others by teaching them. They have strong determination and make great advocates, especially for severely vulnerable people who depend on a social system for survival.

They also makes great teachers and believe in a fair and just society. This is someone who is smarter than the average person and is therefore in an advantageous position. They have more knowledge and skill than the next man so they can handle more stress and opposition. They need to learn to choose their battles more wisely.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Alienating others.

2. A losing battle.

3. Being your own worst enemy.

Meanings

The Seven of Wands reversed indicates inability to hold your ground, especially if it is not popular. There is a lack of defending oneself. The beliefs and actions that should make changes are instead not going anywhere.

Embarrassments and anxiety are suggested. This card also shows up reversed when there is strong opposition from subordinates. There might be a fear of authorities. This card reversed can also mean the querent is fighting a losing battle.

Personality Types

This is someone who is pushing so hard to get their points across that they are losing credibility. The causes they follow are often long-lost and they have little facts to back up their claims. The Seven of Wands reversed people often bring out the worst in others. Their sex drive is often in overdrive and they recklessly seek pleasurable distractions. This is someone who needs stronger boundaries.

They often end up with several divorces and numerous children with their ex-partners. They also have a tendency to lose their jobs, alienate their friends and family, or get themselves into trouble. They are their own worst enemy.

Symbolism

The man holding his wand in a defensive position marks taking a stand. The high ground he stands on illustrates being in the advantage position. His green tunic suggests ambition. The clear sky reflects there are little to no real obstacles or threats to the querent. The man is wearing mismatched footwear, inferring the manâ€™s ability to adapt. He is uneasy in his position, showing he has not yet decided what actions and/or path to take.

Summary

The Seven of Wands is all about being in a advantageous position. People are starting to see you as an authority, whether you are one or not, and it seems like you suddenly have a lot to defend. Joining the crowd is not an option, as you want to be an individual.

Let people know what you are about, and stay proud even if they disapprove. You are here to teach something. Focus on you those who want to learn.

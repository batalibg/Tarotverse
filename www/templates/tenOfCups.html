TEN OF CUPS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Happiness.

2. Spiritual truth.

3. Well-being. Experiencing good fortune.

4. Counting your blessings and feeling grateful.

5. Spiritual connection in your relationships.

6. Being aligned with your true nature. Idealistic values.

7. A happy ending. Having it all.

8. Being in the best relationship you have ever experienced.

Meanings

The Ten of Cups is the card for bliss experienced in your relationships, home and family life. It is about being in alignment with your true nature, and everything in your life being in harmony with your higher self and purpose. The Ten of Cups is the card for perfection and high achievements. Sometimesthough you will have to allow yourself to be ‘human’ and to have imperfections, as those are the qualities that make you ‘real’ and lovable.

In a love reading the Ten of Cups foretells a relationship that will be the best you have ever experienced, primarily due to the spiritual connection and level of perfection in your compatibility. If single, it can mean the querent is waiting for the perfect partner, and will not settle for anything less.

In a career reading the Ten of Cups denotes careers in the public eye, creative jobs or property investments. It can also mean the querent has found the perfect job.

Personality Types

The Ten of Cups character is someone spiritual and positive, but with a tendency to needing everything to be perfect so that they can stay in their blissful state. They work hard towards making others happy so that their own happiness can stay untainted. These people need to have it all: the perfect family, house, job and relationship, and staying on top of everything can be hard work.

They believe in being happily ever after, and will not give up until they have found the life that they are after. This is someone who is seeking a stress free and harmonious life. They don’t deal with toxic people and conflict very well, and disassociate themselves from situations that threaten their heaven on earth. This can turn life into a very limited experience, and they must allow themselves to get a bit dirty without feeling they are no longer a spiritual being.

Behind a seemingly perfect facade lurks hidden hostility and manipulation that this type of person can find hard to deal with. They have strong and idealistic values and are romantic. These people tend to idealize everything, especially their kids and partners, who they put on pedestals.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Falsehood.

2. Disruption in harmony.

3. Hidden agendas.

Meanings

If the Ten of Cups is reversed it alludes to disruption in the harmony, something or someone is making it hard to maintain the love. The querent might be pretending everything is still wonderful in the hope that it will continue forever. Maintaining the happiness takes a lot of effort, and in the end it takes too much work and the cups spill out the joy and happiness. The moral of the Ten of Cups reversed is that less is sometimes more. The harder we try, the less we accomplish.

Personality Types

The Ten of Cups reversed type is someone who’s pursuit for happiness has ironically left them unhappy and unfulfilled. They are lonely people who through their own high standards became too hard to please. They are hard work and they can not be trusted. These people tend to manipulate, and have hidden agendas. They often force their ideals onto others. They never compromise. When they achieve their ideals they find them empty and unsatisfying. Living the dream has become a nightmare and their personal heaven has become a personal hell instead. They have trapped themselves.

They want to be happy and need to execute total control on their environment to contain their happiness. However happiness is not something that can be controlled, nor can it be contained, and if they were self-aware they would understand this. They need to love more unconditionally. The Ten of Cups reversed personalities have a tendency to think that they are perfect and that they have nothing to work on, but this is not the truth.

Symbolism

The nine Cups in an arch, representing abundance and satisfaction. The wooden bench refers to being grounded. The man’s red hat illustrates his passion. The blue fabric equates to health and tranquillity. His yellow shoes serves as a sign that the path he is on is one of happiness, joy and energy.

Summary

The Ten of Cups characterizes spiritual bliss, happiness and perfect memories in the making. There is a renewal happening in your life. Your outer life might not seem to change, but there are inner changes occurring that soon filter through every part of your life. It speaks of positive transformation through inner peace and serenity.

SIX OF WANDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Victory and success.

2. Coming out on top.

3. Feeling good about yourself.

4. Being very fortunate.

5. Being looked up to and admired.

6. Popularity.

7. Getting recognition.

Meanings

The Six of Wands is the card for popularity, progress, victory and success, self-confidence and getting what you want. You will be getting recognition for something that is important to you. People look up to you. The one who seeks shall find.

Move towards your dreams, rather than away from your fears. Your inspiration and motivation will get you further than your doubts and fears ever will.

In a love reading the Six of Wands indicates being adored by your partner.

In a career reading it signifies working on projects that attract recognition.

Personality Types

The Six of Wands persona is someone confident and accomplished. They are very strong and have the ability to succeed against all odds. They are winners in every sense of the word and their friends and family boast about their connection to them. This is someone who has public recognition as well as the adoration of their family. They are optimistic people who are willing to take risks to reach their goals.

They love their family, especially their children, and they know how to show their appreciation to the special people in their lives. In return they are much loved. When things are dark they brighten up the situation and make everything better. This is someone who has a great reputation based on being approachable and friendly.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Unapproachable.

2. Unpopular.

3. Overlooked.

4. Loss of reputation.

5. Defeat.

Meanings

While the Six of Wands is reversed it signifies not getting the rewards that are owed. Delays are indicated. Improvements that should be made are not being made, and instead there is deterioration. Everything is aimed at the lowest common denominator and there is a lack of quality.

Personality Types

The Six of Wands reversed hints at someone who has a tendency to brag and over exaggerate their own abilities. They love attention and expect everyone to give them constant attention and recognition for their achievements. They believe others should show them an unreasonable amount of respect and are shocked and horrified if people don’t bow to their demands.

If people are unaffected by the Six of Wands so-called charm, they think it is something seriously wrong with them. Thesevpeople are stuck up and unappealing, and some even have narcissistic traits.

Symbolism

The wreath around the man’s head stands for victory. The white horse symbolizes being the bearer of a hero, and strengthens the significance on the man riding the horse.

The wand in the man’s left hand has a wreath tied to it, evincing achievements and success. The footmen marks public recognition. The horse’s decorations indicates rewards.

FOUR OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Peace and quiet.

2. Meditation.

3. Rest. Taking time out.

4. The end of a conflict.

5. A place where you can have rest.

6. A placid and calm nature.

7. Getting enough sleep.

8. Finding peace and rejuvenation in a relationship.

Meanings

The Four of Swords is about rest and rejuvenation, getting enough sleep and taking time out to meditate. Sometimes we only get enough rest when we are forced to take time out, so have a look at the other cards in the spread to see if the rest you are getting is due to lifestyle or due to accumulated stress that has collapsed on itself.

In a love reading the Four of Swords can indicate a long term relationship where the couple has grown to be like one person instead of two. If the querent is single it can mean he/she is at peace with being on their own, and may prefer it that way.

In a career reading the Four of Swords signifies administrative work, legal work or a health care worker.

Personality Types

The Four of Swords indicates someone who is calm, non-confrontational and placid. They have learned to get along with almost anyone, and make great partners and friends. Few things upset them. In conflicts they work towards a peaceful resolution that will benefit everyone. They prefer a simple life and avoid busy places and crowds.

This is someone who needs peace and quiet to reflect and gather their thoughts. They seek relief from stress and will not function properly without enough sleep. They love taking breaks where they day-dream and ponder about life. They love spiritual activities.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Restlessness.

2. Burn-outs.

3. Lack of progress.

Meanings

The Four of Swords reversed signals restlessness and burning out. Thoughts and beliefs interrupt the person from recuperating. When the Four of Swords is reversed sometimes people have injuries that prevent them from getting comfortable, or the querent needs constant stimulation to feel at peace. It can also mean there is a lack of progress in the querent's life. Procrastination is indicated.

Personality Types

The Four of Swords reversed indicates someone who is unable to relax. There are often problems in personal relationships. They are always very busy, but get little done. Their minds are full of clutter which is often mirrored in their homes, that are also messy. They long for a good nights sleep and harmonious relationships.

They often settle for less than they deserve, however there is not enough clarity in their minds to deal with issues appropriately. Therefore they often end up staying where they are, feeling ill at ease.

Symbolism

The knight laying on a tomb symbolises rejuvenation. The three Swords pointing downwards represent the resolution of the pain seen in the Three of Swords. The horizontal sword represents the term ‘burying of the hatchet’, conveying past conflicts that have been resolved. The stained glass is decorated with an image of a woman and a child, signifying the birth of new ideas and creativity when the rest period is over.

Summary

When the Four of Swords shows in your cards take a look at the company you keep. You are blessed with someone very special - someone who calms you down to give you peace of mind (though if you get Four of Swords reversed it is the opposite).

The Four of Swords often speak of the types of situations where we have the time to create something incredible. It is very hard to create when we are stressed and out of sorts. Being creative takes up a lot of energy - it is hard work! It is the kind of hard work we can only accomplish when we are meditative and calm. The Four of Swords encourages such an environment.

THE LOVERS

Upright Reversed Astrology Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Love, union and marriage.

2. Being with your soul mate.

3. Feeling passionate about something or someone.

4. Physical attraction.

5. Communication with like-minded people.

6. Doing things that make you feel whole.

7. Being enlightened about what decisions to make.

8. Intimacy.

Meanings

The Lovers card represents love and relationships and soul mates. The Lovers embody physical attraction and the choices we make to become more whole. It's about doing the things that makes us feel complete, and being with the people who make us feel at home. The Lovers card is all about wholeness and union. It is also a card for enlightenment and communication, by being intellectually nourished as well as emotionally fulfilled. To find our match in a partner, job or community we must first get to know ourselves on a deeper level.

The Lovers encourages you to go out there and find yourself, follow your passions, try different things and learn by opposites. Only then can you really know who you are. By doing what you would normally do, you will find out what you do want. By being with someone you are not compatible with you will figure out who you are compatible with. This way you will know what you need and want and you can attract the best partner for yourself.

The Lovers in a love reading denotes being with your soul mate.

In a career question the Lovers often signifies stimulating work such as information gathering and media work.

Personality Types

The Lovers archetype is someone who makes choices from the heart. This is someone who needs to emotionally and intellectually connect to every aspect of their lives. This is someone who puts efforts into the way they look and are passionate and sensual people. They are attractive and passionate. Their appearance and image are important to them, and they care what you think of them. They put an effort into their relationship by being witty, curious and appealing. They often need to be in a relationship to feel whole.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Separation.

2. Disconnection.

3. Unable to make a decision.

4. Making a poor decision.

Meanings

The Lovers reversed portends a breakup between partners, families or friends. Whatever it is, the people who split up are not creating the same magic and chemistry on their own, and become less whole. It can also indicate a wrong choice being made.

The spiritual and mental connection between people is lost or blocked. When this card shows up reversed you might not know what to do. Decisions are being made according to earthly desires and needs, because it is practical, rather than because it makes you happy and fulfilled.

Personality Types

The Lovers reversed type is someone who has difficulty making choices and is often in and out of relationships, unable to make a deep commitment. This is someone who believes there is something wrong with them and has problems in social settings. They might display obsessions about how they look and measure themselves by an external set of impossible standards. They often display erratic and imbalanced behavior. Lovers reversed indicates someone who is cruel to themselves and to those who love them, desperately wanting love and approval from those who will not give it to them.

Astrology

The Lovers is ruled by Gemini, which is ruled by Mercury. It is the third sign of the zodiac and it speaks of the immediate environment: siblings, neighbors, maps, letters, publishing, sales, advertising and communication between people. It speaks of the dualistic side of humanity, male/female, yin/yang, left/right brain. It also speaks of a person's ability to gather and communicate information. Mercury in Gemini has many gifts to offer, especially socially as it promotes curiosity and humor. It is associated with the kind of stimulus that makes you feel whole.

Symbolism

The Lovers meanings are depict through the card's symbolism. The two Lovers symbolize Adam and Eve. Eve is standing in front of the tree of knowledge. The tree of knowledge, the serpent and the apple in one tree represents the garden of Eden, forbidden knowledge and sexuality. The 5 apples stands for the 5 senses. The snake also refers to the senses, as well as the temptations of the world. Adam stands in front of the Tree of Life, a token of divine love.

There are 12 flames representing the 12 signs of the zodiac, the circle of life. The two trees also symbolize pillars, with the mountain forming the third pillar, and a sacred center. Being a mountain it illustrates unfailing loyalty.

The purple cloak expresses royalty. The sun signals happiness derived from enlightenment. The green grass shows fertility and happiness. The man looks at the woman, who looks at the angel, which exhibits the balance between physical desire and spiritual needs.

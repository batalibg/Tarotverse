TWO OF SWORDS

Upright Reversed Symbolism Combinations Summary
Upright

Key Phrases

1. Compromises.

2. Staying calm.

3. Avoiding to rock the boat.

4. Staying out of trouble.

5. Avoiding getting emotionally involved.

6. Being objective and impartial.

7. Being passive in an attempt to keep peace.

8. Avoiding conflicts.

9. Acting uninterested.

Meanings

The Two of Swords is the card for compromise, passiveness, keeping peace, being in a stalemate, indecision or avoiding conflict. The passiveness of this card is temporary, as you will not be in this state forever. You are waiting to see what your options are, bidding your time and waiting for a better result before taking appropriate action. The Two of Swords often shows up when the querent is afraid of showing people what they really stand for, in fear of upsetting others.

The Two of Swords speaks to facts, laws and regulations. It speaks of those things that can be proven and measured, things that can be used as evidence.

In a love reading it suggests the querent wants to be an equal in relationship, and they avoid conflicts to the best of their ability. Sometimes this card hints that the querent is unable to see a suitable partner right in front of them.

In a career reading the Two of Swords indicates legal, peace-keeping or diplomatic work. Two This card intimates cooperation.

Personality Types

This is someone who seeks harmony in relationships and situations. They find it hard to function if there are confrontations and/or unresolved issues causing toxic undercurrents in their environment. They need things to be balanced, and work hard towards keeping and/or restoring peace around them. They do not like conflict, and will compromise a great deal to avoid arguments.

They find it hard to make decisions and try their best not to rock the boat. When faced with changes they think of how it will affect other people more than how it will affect them. It is hard for them to tell others how they feel, they like to keep it to themselves. They are private people.

Reversed

Key Phrases

1. Conflicts.

2. Insults.

3. Unnecessary drama.

Meanings

The Two of Swords reversed says conflicts are unavoidable, and compromises will not be reached. The querent might be exhibiting too much personality, or creates drama just to have something to do. This card often shows up reversed when there is a lot of talk and little action. The person might pretend to know what they are doing, and are faking it until they make it. There is an information overload and confusion.

Tips for when the Two of Swords shows up reversed in a reading: be aware of toxic tongue syndrome. The querent might be on the receiving end, or is the one doing it. Help the querent find ways of engaging in a healthy way with others. Being a team player will get them further than acting uninterested.

Personality Types

This is someone who is unable to separate facts from fiction. They are imbalanced and confused, and they don't know what is going on around them. This person is unable to keep relationships harmonious, and there is always unnecessary drama going on. They don't know who to trust and often end up putting their trust in the wrong people.

The Two of Swords reversed reflects someone who is chaotic and at times confrontational. They lack tact, and are always saying the wrong thing at the wrong time to the wrong people. Sometimes they keep secrets of abuse from those who can help them.

Symbolism

The blindfolded woman represents an inability to see clearly what is going on. The two Swords act as objectivity and impartiality. The two Swords form a cross, referring to strategic thinking. The sea filled with rocks and crags are obstacles to ships, marking difficulties in communicating on an emotional level. The waxing moon equates to a time for conceptualized ideas.

Summary

The Two of Swords encourages staying as objective and detached as possible. In a career question it is a good card to get, as it implies you have the skills to get the job done, and that you will be learning new skills with ease.

This card signifies staying open to new ideas. On a more negative note, the querent will have problems holding on to a belief system even if it will be beneficial for them. They are slow to believe and quick to doubt.
